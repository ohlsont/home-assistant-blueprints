- Disconnect power before any service or maintenance.
- The machine must be vented and the safety switch locked before service.
- Service and maintenance must only be performed by qualified personnel.
- Filter replacement and system pressure maintenance can be done by the user.

## 2. System Description

### 2.2 How It Works

The Qvantum-ETK is an exhaust-air heat pump (franluftsvärmepump). It extracts heat from indoor exhaust air:

1. **A** - Indoor air is drawn into the house duct system.
2. **B** - The exhaust air is led to the Qvantum-ETK.
3. **C** - The ETK provides heating and hot water to the house.
4. **D** - Air is expelled after passing through the ETK (cooled, as energy has been extracted).
5. **E** - Fresh air enters through supply air vents, drawn in by the slight negative pressure created by the exhaust system.

### 2.3 Functional Principle

The room-temperature exhaust air passes through a filter to the evaporator. The ETK has a specially designed diffuser distributing airflow evenly over the evaporator for maximum energy recovery. The refrigerant (R-134a) evaporates at low boiling point, absorbing energy from the air. It is then compressed (raising temperature significantly), passed through the condenser where it releases energy to the heating water circuit, and then through the expansion valve where pressure and temperature drop. The cycle repeats.

The heat pump distributes heat via a diverter valve (växelventil) to either radiator heating or domestic hot water. During very cold weather and high hot water demand, the compressor's heat production is supplemented by the electric backup heater (elpatron) as needed.

The system is Legionella-safe as no hot water is stored. Fresh tap water is heated on demand by flowing through finned channels in the heated accumulator tank.

## 3. Installation

### 3.2 Placement

- Place against outer wall or insulated partition wall, on a solid floor (preferably concrete).
- Floor drain for condensate and safety valve discharge must be nearby.
- Minimum 10 mm clearance from walls and furniture.
- Minimum ceiling height: 2150-2200 mm (depending on vent connection).
- Minimum 900 mm free space in front for service access.

### 3.3 Radiator System

- The radiator system must be properly balanced for even heat distribution.
- Ensure return temperature does not exceed 48C at the lowest calculated outdoor temperature.
- These adjustments should only be made by the installer in the SET-UP menu (chapter 6).

### 3.4 Floor Heating System

- The ETK is designed for floor heating systems.
- Return temperature should be adjusted down to max 35C.
- If the floor material requires a different temperature, this must be taken into account.
- Adjustments only via installer in SET-UP menu.

### 3.5 Combined System (Radiators + Floor Heating)

- Radiator operation set to max 48C.
- Floor heating controlled via separate shunt groups.
- Adjustments only via installer in SET-UP menu.

### 3.6 Supply Air Unit Connection

- Requires its own separate circulation pump and thermostat valve.
- When connected, the supply air temperature setting must be 3-5C lower than the room temperature setpoint (BOR-värde).

### 3.7 External Room Temperature Sensor

If the ventilation duct temperature may give incorrect values for the heat pump's control (e.g., when a tumble dryer or drying cabinet is connected to the duct system), an external room temperature sensor should be installed.

- Compatible sensor type: **Pt100**
- Place centrally in the house, approximately 1.5 m above floor level.
- Connect to terminal block **pins 7, 8, and 9** (pins 8 and 9 bridged).
- The built-in temperature sensor must be disconnected from the same terminal block.
- Use a 3-conductor cable with minimum cross-section 0.75 mm2.

### 3.8 Pipe Dimensions

| Connection | Type | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Heating supply & return | Copper pipe | 22 mm |
| Hot water | Copper pipe | 22 mm |
| Cold water | Copper pipe | 22 mm |
| Condensate/spillwater | Copper pipe | 22 mm |

### 3.10 Circulation Pump

- Self-adjusting capacity based on system demand.
- 3 capacity ranges switchable via the pump's coupling cover switch.
- Delta T on the circulation pump is minimum 3-5C.
- The system must be thoroughly vented before operation.

### 3.11 Ventilation Connection

- Connect to ductwork with flexible hoses.
- Duct system must be airtightness class B.
- The entire exhaust duct must be diffusion-tight (minimum PE30 or equivalent).

### 3.12 Electrical Connection

- 400 V (3-phase + N + PE) with multi-conductor 2.5 mm2 cable (4 mm2 at max backup power).
- Main switch on front, plus external circuit breaker on wall nearby.
- Fuse: 16 A for all models.
- Earth fault breaker: minimum 300 mA (due to normal leakage currents).

### 3.13 Earth Fault Breaker

- Must be minimum 300 mA.
- Do not use lower values, as normal leakage currents may trip the breaker.
- Ensure the system is filled with water and fully vented BEFORE connecting power.

### 3.14 Relay Mounting & Backup Heater Limitation

- After filling and venting, mount relays as shown in the manual.
- Electric backup stages: Relay 1 = 1 kW, Relay 2 = 2 kW, Relay 3 = 2 kW.
- For BBR compliance, limit the backup heater by mounting relays for only the number of stages matching the maximum allowed installed power.

## 4. Commissioning

### 4.2 Before Start

- Indoor temperature should be at least 17C at startup for the heat pump to produce sufficient energy and complete defrost cycles.
- If below 17C, use an electric fan heater or run the heat pump in emergency mode until temperature rises above 17C.

### 4.3 Filling the HVAC System

1. Open all radiator valves (leave open during operation).
2. Open the tank's vent valve at the bottom.
3. Open the fill valve under the tank on the cold water side.
4. Close the vent valve when water starts flowing through.
5. Fill until pressure gauge shows 1.0 bar (cold water).
6. Target pressure: 1.0-1.5 bar at operating temperature.

### 4.4 Venting the Heating System

- Critical: all radiators and floor heating loops must be completely vented and open.
- The condenser (highest point) has a vent at the top of the heat pump.
- Air in the condenser reduces performance and can cause alarms with stoppages.

### 4.5 Venting the Tank

- Vent the hot water tank during filling.
- Additional venting may be needed a few days after the pump has been running.

### 4.6 Ventilation Adjustment

- Must be done after installation for the heat pump to function as expected.
- Sets minimum outdoor air intake, directly affecting energy consumption.
- Start with fan at a medium setting (~150 m2 house area), adjust slightly higher for larger houses and lower for smaller.
- Adjustment should be done within about a week after startup.

Fan speed guide:

| House area | Fan % | Speed setting |
|---|---|---|
| 280 m2 | 100% | 10 |
| 250 m2 | 85% | 9 |
| 200 m2 | 70% | 8 |
| 150 m2 | 50% | 7 |
| 120 m2 | 40% | 6 |
| 100 m2 | 35% | 5 (min) |

### 4.7 Starting

1. Turn on all fuses in the fuse box.
2. Turn on any external main switch.
3. Turn on the heat pump's main switch.
4. Set room temperature (e.g., 20C).
5. Set hot water temperature (e.g., 55-60C).
6. The pump starts in hot water mode (prioritized), then begins space heating.

Note: All values that increase compressor output are delayed by ~3 minutes. Values that decrease output take effect immediately. This ensures smooth regulation.

## 5. Information During Operation

### 5.2 General

- The Qvantum-ETK is designed for minimal user interaction.
- Radiator/floor heating system must be filled to correct pressure and fully vented.
- All radiator valves should be fully open at startup.
- The ventilation must be adjusted by a professional.

### 5.3 Removing the Front Panel

- Press the lower part of the front panel (~30 cm from floor) from one side toward the other, pull outward, and lift up.
- The front is secured with a spring hook that must be released from the frame.

### 5.4 Power Switch

- ON (I): heat pump is on, automatic startup of entire system.
- OFF (0): unit is off. Note: parts of the heat pump remain live even when switched off.
- To fully de-energize: switch off external main breaker or all fuses.

### 5.5 Emergency Operation

If the control unit fails:
1. Turn off the main switch.
2. Flip the power switch on top of the electric backup terminal to emergency mode.
3. Turn the main switch back to ON.
4. Only space heating is produced (electric backup only). Call service.

The thermostat is factory-set with overheat protection at 96C. The power switch must be ON during emergency operation.

### 5.6 Alarms

- **Filter alarm**: audible alarm, red LED, text message when filter needs replacement.
- **High return temperature alarm**: text message + orange LED. Not an acute alarm, just a warning. If persistent (>1 day), investigate: air in radiator system, circulation pump fault, dirty radiator filter, closed radiator elements.
- **Red LED alarm**: requires service. Call installer.

### 5.7 Ventilation Filter Replacement

- Filter type: Class G4.
- Replace 3-5 times per year depending on dust levels.
- The heat pump can be damaged if operated without a filter for extended periods. Warranty void if operated without filter.
- Built-in sensor triggers alarm when filter is dirty. Alarm sounds and LED flashes until new filter is installed.
- Always keep at least one spare filter at home.

### 5.8 Condensate Pipe Check

- Check the condensate pipe function when changing filter.
- The pipe can become clogged, leading to leaks. Flush the condensate pipe and ensure all debris is removed.

### 5.9 System Pressure Check

- Check pressure gauge regularly.
- Cold system (10-25C): above 1.0 bar.
- Warm system: 1.0-1.5 bar.
- Refill if needed via the fill valve behind the coupling under the tank.

### 5.10 Resetting Electric Backup Overheat Protection

- Trips at 96C.
- Reset by inserting a cross-head screwdriver into the circular hole on top of the protection box and pressing firmly. A strong click confirms reset.
- Located in the upper left corner of the lower section, above the circulation pump.

## 6. Panel & Control Unit

### 6.1 Overview

The control unit (touchscreen) is on the front right side. Users can adjust:

- **Indoor temperature** (BOR-värde): default 20C
- **Hot water temperature**: default 55C (recommended range 50-58C)
- **External heat pump on/off** (tillskott)
- **Electric backup on/off** (eltillskott)

All other values are read-only for users. Changing other values voids the warranty unless authorized by the manufacturer in writing.

### 6.2 Adjusting Indoor Temperature

The home screen shows:
- Left value = **AR-värde** (actual/current temperature)
- Right value with +/- buttons = **BOR-värde** (setpoint/target temperature)

The arrow on the left indicates the current operating mode (house icon = space heating, water tap = hot water).

Adjust using +/- buttons or tap the value field to open a numeric keypad.

Values must be confirmed with OK. If not confirmed, the pump reverts to the original value after a moment.

### 6.3 Adjusting Hot Water Temperature

- Recommended range: 50-58C.
- Higher value = more hot water capacity but higher energy consumption.
- Note: high calcium water may cause limescale at high temperatures.

### 6.4 External Heat Pump On/Off

- Via SET-UP menu in the top row.
- Can toggle Ext. VP (external heat pump) on/off for Room and Water separately.

### 6.5 Electric Backup On/Off

- Via SET-UP menu.
- Can toggle each electric stage (Elsteg 1, 2, 3) on/off individually for Room and Water.

### 6.6 Installer Settings (Installer Only)

Access: SET-UP > Installningar > Login: User = Administratör, Code = 123.

**Installer settings screen:**

| Parameter | Default | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Defrost interval | 3600 s | Time between defrost cycles |
| Defrost max time | 600 s | Maximum defrost duration |
| Defrost temp clear | 12C | Temperature at which defrost is considered complete |
| Return line max temp | 48C | Maximum return water temperature (radiators) |
| Hot water hysteresis | - | - |
| Compressor | 3.0C | - |
| Elsteg 1 threshold | 5.0C | Outdoor temp below which electric stage 1 activates |
| Elsteg 2 threshold | 6.0C | Outdoor temp below which electric stage 2 activates |
| Elsteg 3 threshold | 7.0C | Outdoor temp below which electric stage 3 activates |
| Space heating from | 1.0C | Degrees above setpoint at which heating starts |

- For floor heating: max temp should be adjusted to 35C.
- "Husvärme fran" (space heating from) = number of degrees above the setpoint (BOR-värde).

### 6.7 Status Screen

Accessible via the magnifying glass button on the home screen. Shows:
- Room temperature, water temperature, evaporator temperature, return temperature
- Compressor speed (%)
- External heat pump status, electric stages status
- Defrost max time
- Frequency converter status (alarm list, parameter list, monitor)

### 6.8 Alarms

- After installation, it may take time for all air to be vented. This can cause the circulation pump to not circulate effectively.
- High return temperature may occur if the radiator/floor heating loop returns hotter water than the system's limits, or if installed with another heat source (e.g., water-jacketed wood stove).
- The increased gas pressure causes the compressor to work harder. After several attempts to reduce the compressor speed, the pump will alarm with "omriktarlarm" (inverter alarm).
- This is not an acute alarm but a signal to address the high return temperature. After fixing the cause, reset the alarm in the alarm menu.
